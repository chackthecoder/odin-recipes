Steak Recipe

Very Well Done

Steak is a simple yet indulgent dish made by grilling or pan-searing cuts of beef to perfection. It can be seasoned with just salt and pepper or enhanced with marinades and herbs, allowing the natural flavors of the meat to shine through. Different cuts, such as ribeye, filet mignon, or sirloin, each offer their own unique texture and taste, from tender and juicy to bold and robust

Cooking steak to the desired doneness, whether rare, medium, or well-done, allows for a personalized dining experience. Often served with sides like mashed potatoes, grilled vegetables, or a fresh salad, steak makes for a hearty and satisfying meal. Finished with a pat of butter or a splash of sauce, steak is a versatile dish perfect for both casual dinners and special occasions.

ingredients

  • 1 pound sweet Italian sausage
  • ¾ pound lean ground beef
  • ½ cup minced onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 (28 ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 (6.5 ounce) cans canned tomato sauce
  • 2 (6 ounce) cans tomato paste
  • ½ cup water
  • 2 tablespoons white sugar
  • 4 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley, divided
  • 1 ½ teaspoons dried basil leaves
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt, divided, or to taste
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • ½ teaspoon fennel seeds
  • ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 16 ounces ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg
  • ¾ pound mozzarella cheese, sliced
  • ¾ cup grated Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Prepare the Sauce: Cook ground beef or sausage with onions and garlic. Add tomato sauce, tomato paste, and season with salt, pepper, basil, and oregano. Let it simmer.
  2. Cook the Lasagna Noodles: Boil lasagna noodles until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  3. Prepare the Cheese Mixture: Mix ricotta cheese with eggs, Parmesan, parsley, salt, and pepper.
  4. Layer the Lasagna: Spread a layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a baking dish. Add a layer of noodles, then spread the ricotta mixture, followed by shredded mozzarella cheese. Repeat the layers, finishing with a layer of meat sauce and mozzarella on top.
  5. Bake: Cover with foil and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25-30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes until the cheese is golden and bubbly.
  6. Rest and Serve: Let the lasagna rest for 10-15 minutes before serving.
